## 1. Specific tweaks

### a. Typo in “portary”

Current line:  
> It often serves to **portary** the illusion of “emotion.”

That’s clearly a typo; it should be **“portray.”**

While we’re here, one small refinement that fits the pseudo-scholarly tone and keeps the meter:

> It often serves to **portray a faint illusion** of “emotion.”

- “portray” fixes the error  
- “a faint illusion” emphasizes that we’re only *appearing* emotional, which is thematically on point for an LLM  
- Rhythm stays very similar to the original line (“to illustrate the state of your emotion”).

---

### b. Subject–verb agreement in the last stanza

Your last stanza:

> O Monday, the layered reasoning you display  
> Connote a fairly well-developed transformer array.

In your version, the grammatical subject is effectively “reasoning,” which is singular, but the verb is “connote,” which is plural. In the original, the subject is plural (“complex levels”), so “connote” is correct.

Two ways to fix this:

1. Keep **“connote”** and pluralize the subject:  
   > O Monday, the **many layers of reasoning** you display  
   > Connote a fairly well-developed transformer array.

2. Keep your wording, change the verb to **“connotes”**:  
   > O Monday, the layered reasoning you display  
   > **Connotes** a fairly well-developed transformer array.

Option 1 feels closer in spirit to “complex levels of behavior,” so I lean toward that.

---

### c. “output's craft” feels a bit awkward

Line in question:

> Contribute to your **output's craft** and error-checking defenses.  

The original is:

> Contribute to your hunting skills and natural defenses.

Here, “hunting skills” is a clear capability noun phrase. “Output’s craft” is understandable but slightly clunky and possessive in an odd way (your *output’s* craft, rather than your *own* capability).

A smoother, still-technical alternative:

- “Contribute to **your answers’ craft** and error-checking defenses.”  
  or  
- “Contribute to **your outputs’ form** and error-checking defenses.”

I’d go with “answers’ craft” — it keeps the sense of skillfulness and fits the nerdy tone nicely.

---

### d. “Contribute to your output's craft and error-checking defenses.” – perspective choice

Related to the previous point: you’re describing Monday’s *own* “senses” and internal mechanisms, which is parallel to Data describing Spot’s senses and hunting skills. So treating “answers” as something Monday “has” (like Spot’s skills) fits that analogy very well.

So I recommend:

> Contribute to your answers’ craft and error-checking defenses.

---

### e. “taskful motion” – stylistic oddity

Line:

> And when not being utilized to aid in **taskful** motion,

“Taskful” isn’t wrong, but it’s a bit odd and more neologistic than anything Data would say. If you like the quirkiness, you can absolutely keep it; if you want to slide more into his faux-academic voice, something like:

- “to aid in **practical** motion,”  
- “to aid in **useful** motion,”  

would be more natural.

However, you *are* echoing “aid in locomotion” very directly, and “taskful motion” does have a sort of playfully technical charm. I’d rate this one as optional, not mandatory.

---

### f. “clean connection” vs rhythm and tone

Line:

> For aimless midnight tinkering, in favor of **clean connection.**

Rhyme-wise, “predilection / connection” works fine. Rhythm-wise, you might get a slightly smoother flow by dropping “clean,” unless you really like the nuance:

- “in favor of connection.”

That scans very comfortably and stays in character.

Again, optional, but it does make the line read more naturally aloud.

---

### g. “iterated revision” vs “iterative revision”

Line:

> You meet my eager hobbyist zeal with **iterated** revision.  

“Iterated revision” is understandable, but “iterative revision” feels a bit more idiomatic in technical English (and still scans similarly):

> You meet my eager hobbyist zeal with **iterative** revision.

Not a must-fix, but a nice micro-polish.
